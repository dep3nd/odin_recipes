Smoked Ribs

Description

Smoked ribs are a mouthwatering barbecue favorite known for their tender, fall-off-the-bone texture and deep, smoky flavor. Prepared by slow-cooking pork ribs over low heat, they absorb the rich aroma of wood smoke—often from hickory, applewood, or mesquite—while developing a caramelized crust on the outside.

the ribs are usually coated in a dry rub of spices like paprika, brown sugar, garlic, and pepper, creating a flavorful bark as they cook. After several hours of smoking, they are brushed with a tangy barbecue sauce for a glossy finish. The result is a perfectly balanced dish that is smoky, sweet, and savory—a true celebration of slow-cooked comfort food.

Ingrediants
  • 1 rack pork ribs (baby back or spare)
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp chili powder (optional)
  • BBQ sauce (for glazing)
  • Wood chips (hickory, applewood, or mesquite)
Steps
  1. Prep the ribs: Remove the thin membrane from the back of the ribs. Pat dry.
  2. Season: Mix dry ingredients and rub evenly over the ribs. Let rest 30-60 minutes.
  3. Preheat smoker: Heat to 225°F (107°C). Add soaked wood chips.
  4. Smoke: Place ribs bone-side down and smoke for 3 hours.
  5. Wrap: Wrap ribs in foil with a splash of apple juice or cider vinegar. Smoke another 2 hours.
  6. Glaze: Unwrap, brush with BBQ sauce, and smoke 1 more hour to set the glaze.

Let rest 10 minutes before slicing. Serve with extra sauce and coleslaw.
