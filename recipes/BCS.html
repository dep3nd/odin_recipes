Backed Chicken Schnitzel

Home

Description

Baked chicken schnitzel is a lighter twist on the classic fried favorite, offering the same crispy texture with less oil. Tender chicken breasts are pounded thin, then coated in seasoned flour, dipped in beaten egg, and covered with a layer of golden breadcrumbs.

Instead of frying, the schnitzels are baked in a hot oven until crisp on the outside and juicy inside. A light drizzle of olive oil helps them achieve a beautiful crunch without excess grease.

Served fresh from the oven with a squeeze of lemon and a side of salad, potatoes, or steamed vegetables, baked chicken schnitzel makes a delicious, wholesome meal that is both satisfying and simple to prepare.

Ingrediants

  • Chicken breasts (pounded thin)
  • Flour
  • Eggs (beaten)
  • Breadcrumbs (plain or panko)
  • Olive oil or melted butter
  • Salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment and lightly oil it.
  2. Prepare coating: set up three bowls — one with flour, one with beaten eggs, one with breadcrumbs mixed with seasonings.
  3. Coat chicken: dredge each piece in flour, dip in egg, then coat with breadcrumbs.
  4. Arrange on tray and drizzle or spray lightly with oil.
  5. Bake for 15-20 minutes, flipping halfway, until golden and cooked through (internal temp 165°F / 74°C).
  6. Serve with lemon wedges and a side like salad, mashed potatoes, or coleslaw.
